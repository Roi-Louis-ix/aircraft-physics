Drag

Another force created by the wing is drag, which resists motion. It is caused by the movement of any object through a fluid. There are three types of drag, which are skin friction drag, form drag, and wave drag.

Skin friction drag is the friction between the object's surface and the fluid around it, whereof the fluid is air in our situation. Form drag is the drag arising from the object's shape. Wave drag is caused by shock waves, which are formed as part of an object travels at supersonic speeds.

Flaps

During takeoff and landing extra lift is needed. This could be done simply by increasing the wing's surface area, which will increase lift as shown in its equation above. But increasing area will also increase drag as shown in the drag equation above. Therefore, a retractable method of increasing the wing's surface area when more lift is needed. Flaps are thence used.
